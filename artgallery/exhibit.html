Group Exhibition curated by Tammy Tran

EXHIBITIONS

Marcel Duchamp
"Fountain", 1917

Duchamp's "Fountain" had came to mind immediately when I had begun this project from what I had learned of it previously. With the self-aware theme that this particular piece gives with critiquing art galleries and what is considered fine art, I found it an important piece to incorporate in my theme. In its history, this piece of art was very known for its landmark in modern art. This piece had left a strong impression on many: including myself. With this in mind, I found this piece to be a perfect part of my group curated exhibition.
Norman Rockwell
"The Problem We All Live With", 1964

Rockwell's "The Problem We All Live With" was a piece that stood out to me immediately. With how strong the subject of the piece is, the very fact that there is historical context of an actual black child going through the same events being portrayed here stood out to me. The artist isn't afraid to portray heavy subjects, even despite the backlash that the artist had faced after creating this work. The feelings that this black girl had felt after growing up, despite never even meeting the artist behind the piece, of how it inspired her made me very inspired to want to put this piece as one of my exhibits. I think that message this artist brings to this girl as well as the audience is a fantastic reflection of this group exhibition's message.
Banksy
"Royal Courts of Justice Mural", 2025

Banksy's mural had left a strong impression on me when I had first seen it being mentioned online. The location that this mural was placed in, the Royal Courts of Justice in London, was a very bold message on Banksy's part with what the subject manner of the artwork was. Even despite the attempts of the artwork being erased, the chilling remains of the artwork still left a strong impression on many including myself. It does evidently show that even despite the attempts to hide a message, especially in the modern age with technology, that these messages will never go hidden. The fact that Banksy is known as an anonymous artist with only a pseudonym to note himself makes the message even more bold, as people with a lot of legal power are directly confronted without any face or real name they can go to in order to punish Banksy for his actions.
Yoko Ono
"Cut Piece", 1964

Ono's performance art had stood out a lot for me and has been a strong memory left in my mind even long after I had first learned about her performance. The clear display of human nature and how people will show apathy towards others when given the power to is an unsettling thing to witness firsthand. Especially in modern day, it feels as if this apathetic behavior towards others displays itself even more with the treatment of women and other minorities. While this piece isn't explicitly trying to stand up against a specific subject, the sheer display of vulnerability that Ono had to be willing to open up with sends a message that clearly stays ingrained in people's minds, even longer after the performance is finished. The message of this piece that was left in my mind is the exact sort of realization that I want to bring to the rest of my audience, and I hope this piece gives the same message that it had to me.
Tracey Emin
"Everyone I Have Ever Slept With", 1995

When looking at Tracy Emin's works, I originally had plans to select another one of her pieces that may have worked for this group exhibition. However, the exposure that Emin had to give when uncovering the names of both her family members as well as the people who had hurt her sexually is a strong message to leave behind. While the name itself may leave off connotations of only sexual encounters, it is clear that there is more then just that in this tent. The intimacy of the tent itself stands out to me as well, the delibrate selection of having you go into a tent that people often sleep with to uncover the names of the people this artist had slept with stood out to me greatly. With that sort of message and intimacy in mind, I believed that this piece critiqued how people view sexuality as a whole in a very compelling manner.
Jens Haaning
"Take the Money and Run", 2021

While this artwork doesn't appear to mean anything from first glance, I believe the history of this artwork represents a protest for the working class effectively. It certainly left a message for the gallery that had paid Haaning for the commissioned artworks. It had so much to the point that legal action had to be taken just to get the money back. I found the fact that legal matters had to be taken to be a great statement in of itself even past the artwork, thus fitting the premise of making a statement well. One could debate that this piece does not mean much, but it certainly puts in question (at least to me) how much art should really be valued as it is very much a luxury.
Ai Weiwei
"Study of Perspective", 1995 - 2003

Weiwei's works left a very strong impression on me as soon as I had been introduced to this artist. I found the very blunt approaches to critiquing the government to be very powerful. Even though the government wants to silence people, especially someone to the likes of Weiwei, it is clear that he wouldn't go down without a fight. That sort of fighting energy is something I wish to inspire in others, even when something like a middle finger like in this piece as well as the other pieces in this collection are associated with being crude. I hope that others, just like myself, are inspired by Weiwei's determination to be heard.
Lori Larusso
"Midden", 2025

Larusso's works all particularly intrigued me, but I enjoyed "Midden" and the very clear messaging from the piece. I find the simplistic colors and shapes, yet the clear messaging of how pollution affects wildlife strong. I had wanted to cover a critique upon the enviornment and how economic spending pollutes it, and I feel like Larusso's artwork represents that very well. Even in the subtle style that Larusso uses, it feels corporate with its simplification. This corporation-esque style adds to the idea that these same businesses harms the environment and the animals that live alongside us on the planet very well.
